#plotting using ggplot : dataset, aesthetics, axis labels and binwidth assigning
ggplot(data=chi)+
  geom_histogram(aes(x=Trip.Duration/60,  y=(..count..)*100/sum(..count..)),fill='gray65',col="gray40", binwidth=50)+ 
  labs(title="Chicago Bike Trip Durations",
       x="Trip Duration in Minutes",
       y="Percent of Total"
       )+
  scale_y_continuous(breaks=seq(0,100,20))+
  scale_x_continuous(breaks=seq(0,500,100))

SUMMARY 1

This graph plots the duration of the trips as a percentage of total durations for Chicago city.

We see that around 90 percent of the bike ride durations are under 100 minutes, which is an important asset or a kpi used in business analytics.

One can preassume the rides to finish in this way for most other cities thereby useful for prediction capabilities.

#Data analysis using dplyr : dataset, top n(where n=20) and descending order arrangement
ny%>%
  group_by(Start.Station) %>%
  tally() %>%
  top_n(20) %>%
  arrange(desc(n)) %>%
  mutate(n=n/100,Start.Station=fct_reorder(Start.Station,n,last)) %>%
  
#plotting using ggplot : dataset, aesthetics, axis labels and grouping and arranging by maximum Start Stations

  ggplot()+
  geom_point(aes(y=Start.Station, x=n), fill='gray10', col="gray40")+
  geom_text(aes(y=Start.Station, x=n,label=round(n,1), hjust=-0.3), col="gray25", size=4.5)+
  labs(title="Top 20 most popular starting bike share locations in NYC",
       x="Popularity Measure",
       y="Stations"
)

Selecting by n

This chart plots the top 20 most popular bike starting stations.

Post calculating the popularity measure which is nothing but a subsidized measure of the number of times a starting station appears in the dataset. This measure is used to plot against the various station names. This is an important asset in determing as to which station more focus and business can be expected and thereby improving overall efficiency.

The chart clearly says that Pershing Square North is the most popular start station with a vast gap between its predecessor.
